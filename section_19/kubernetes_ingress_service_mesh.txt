3. Service Mesh
It acts as a proxy between the client and the service. (Sidecar)
https://istio.io/

A service mesh has a crucial role in managing service-to-service traffic within Kubernetes clusters.
It differentiates between north-south traffic, managed by ingress controllers, and east-west traffic, which
involves communication between microservices within the cluster.

A service mesh acts as a dedicated infrastructure layer that ensures reliable communication among microservices,
offering features like security, observability, and reliability for east-west traffic. It creates a firewall-like
environment by not exposing internal microservices to the outside world, which enhances security.

Using a service mesh allows developers to separate business logic from non-functional requirements, simplifying
the development process. By adopting the sidecar proxy pattern, where a sidecar container accompanies each microservice,
developers can leverage various technologies without compromising the separation of concerns. Example of sidecar container is Istio.
https://istio.io/latest/about/service-mesh/
